The circle

Eight to twelve people. One question. One hour. The circle is the heart of the practice — where a faith stops being an idea and becomes the people in the room with you.

what it is

A small room, once a week

A circle is a small, steady group that meets once a week, online or in person. The same faces, the same hour. It isn't therapy and it isn't a class. It's simply a room where you get to be a whole person, and so does everyone else.

Each week there's a single question. Everyone who wants to speak is heard; no one runs the room. The quietest voice carries the same weight as the loudest. That's the entire shape of it.

The measure we return to: does the quietest, newest, most different person here feel as held as anyone else?

how it works

Four things we keep

01
8–12 people
Large enough for range, small enough that no one disappears. When a circle outgrows the room, it gently becomes two.
02
One question
A single prompt each week. The question carries the hour — you only have to answer it honestly, or sit with it.
03
One hour
It begins on time and ends on time. Honoring the hour is how we honor everyone who showed up to share it.
04
No one presides
Someone keeps time; no one leads. There's no guru, no host with the answers. The roles move around the circle, week to week.
