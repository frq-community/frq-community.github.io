The practice · weekly

The circle

8–12 people. One question. One hour. No agenda, no hierarchy, no performance. The whole thing Frq is built around — showing up for each other, on a schedule, on purpose.

What it is

A small room, once a week

A circle is a small, consistent group that meets once a week — online or in person. Same people, same hour. Not a support group, not a class, not a networking thing. Just a room where you get to be a person and so does everyone else.

Each week there's one question. Everyone gets space to answer if they want to. Nobody has to perform, nobody runs the room, and the quietest person matters as much as the loudest. That's the whole format.

The health check is simple: does the quietest, newest, most different person in the room feel as safe as anyone else?

How it works

Four rules, basically

01
8–12 people
Big enough for range, small enough that everyone gets heard. New circles split when they outgrow the room.
02
One question
A single prompt per week. No agenda beyond it. The question does the work — you just answer honestly or pass.
03
One hour
Starts on time, ends on time. Respecting the hour is how you respect everyone who showed up for it.
04
No hierarchy
Someone keeps time, but nobody leads. No guru, no host with all the answers. Roles rotate. That's the point.
